About · GameDrawer · Vol. I

A calm drawer of classic games.

GameDrawer is a local-first iPhone home for eight classic logic and word games. It exists because most of the modern versions of these games have been hollowed out - and the originals deserved better than that.

Open Solitaire, Sudoku, Minesweeper, or a Merge clone on the App Store today and you'll usually meet the same wall: a banner ad, a forced video ad after every level, fake coins to spend, energy meters that lock you out, paywalls in front of basic features, and a tutorial that won't let you skip. GameDrawer inverts every one of those decisions. The first launch drops you straight into a game. Your stats live on your device. There is no account to make, no email to verify, no upsell to dismiss.

Why GameDrawer exists

The honest reason: classic logic games are some of the best mobile-sized entertainment ever shipped, and almost every modern version of them has been hollowed out by ad networks. The mechanics are still good. The boards are still beautiful. What got broken is everything wrapped around them.

GameDrawer was built to remove every one of those failures. Tap to play. No popups. No paywalls. No tracking. No required network connection. A clean cabinet of games that opens fast, runs offline, and lets the actual game be the thing you remember.

The eight games

Minesweeper

The full classic, properly tuned. Three difficulties - Easy 9×9/10 mines, Medium 16×16/40, Hard 16×30/99. First-tap safety is guaranteed: your first reveal will never land on a mine. Empty cells flood-fill correctly. Tap to reveal, long-press to flag.

Merge

A 4×4 tile-merger in two modes: Win (reach 2048 to win) and Infinite (keep going as long as you can). Score and personal best tracked per mode.

Nonogram

Fill in the squares that match the row and column hints to reveal a picture. Four grid sizes from 5×5 to 20×20. Mark squares you know are empty so you don't lose track. Every puzzle you solve gets saved as a thumbnail in a gallery with your best clear time.

Sudoku

9×9 classic logic with 6,000 curated puzzles across Easy, Medium, Hard, and Extreme. Pencil notes, single-step undo, and two modes: Free (wrong placements show in red, you keep going) or Lives (three mistakes ends the game).

FreeCell

8-column tableau, 4 free cells, 4 foundations. Multi-card sequence moves follow the standard supermove rule. Enter any deal number from 1 to 32,000, or pick a difficulty tier at random. Loss detection when no legal moves remain.

Klondike Solitaire

7-column tableau with a stock and waste pile. Three difficulty modes: Easy (Draw 1), Medium (Draw 2), Hard (Draw 3). Drag cards and sequences, double-tap to send to the foundation, and auto-complete sweeps the board when the position is clear.

Five Letter

Guess a five-letter word in six tries. Each guess colors every tile - green for the right letter in the right spot, yellow for a letter that belongs elsewhere - and the on-screen keyboard tracks what you've ruled out. A new Daily word for everyone, plus Unlimited for as many rounds as you want. Guesses are checked against a 15,000-word list, answers drawn from a pool of 1,000 common words.

Word Grid

Trace a path through adjacent letters on a 4×4 board to build words. Swipe across tiles to spell, and the longer the word the more it scores. Two modes: Timed against the clock or Relaxed with no pressure. Found words stay listed as you play. Every board is generated to guarantee a fair set of findable words.

Features across every game

Video Mode

Every game supports Picture-in-Picture. Pick one of six positions for your video window - large top, large bottom, or four corner slots - and each game reflows its layout to keep every control clear and tappable.

Stats

Best times per difficulty, total games played, win percentage, and a full record table across all eight games. Stored locally via SwiftData so they survive restarts and OS updates. JSON export ships with a versioned schema.

34-preset theme catalog

Every game adapts to the theme you pick. Six DesignKit families - Classic, Sweet, Bright, Soft, Moody, Loud - plus a custom palette editor. The default is "Chrome Diner" (cream + teal + diner red + brushed gunmetal), the same palette used on this site.

Save state

All eight games save your session when you leave. Come back and a resume prompt appears. Decline and you start fresh - nothing is lost permanently.

Optional iCloud sync

Sign in with Apple unlocks CloudKit sync across your devices via the iCloud.com.lauterstar.gamekit private container. Off by default. Sign out and your records stay on the device. There is no Lauterstar-owned server in the picture either way.

Haptics and animations

Polished feedback on flag, reveal, win, and loss across every game. All toggleable in Settings. Quiet by default on Silent. Respects Reduce Motion.

On the way

Flow, Pattern Memory, and Chess puzzles remain on the roadmap. Each one ships when it can stand on its own.

Who GameDrawer is for

  • People who used to play Minesweeper or Solitaire and stopped because the modern versions are unbearable.
  • People who care about a clean look. 34 themes, custom palettes, the same design identity across the Lauterstar catalog.
  • Privacy-minded players. No accounts, no analytics, no servers we own. Optional iCloud is end-to-end through Apple.
  • iPhone owners on iOS 17 or later who want games that respect their time and their battery.

GameDrawer is probably not for: people who want infinite-scroll match-3 dopamine, social leaderboards, daily-streak guilt, or live-service game economies.

The privacy posture

GameDrawer collects no analytics on your gameplay. There are no third-party SDKs reporting back to ad networks. There is no server we operate that holds your records. The optional iCloud sync uses Apple's CloudKit running under your own Apple ID. The Apple privacy manifest declares no collected data and no tracking. Read the full Privacy Policy for specifics.

Frequently asked questions

Is GameDrawer really free?

Yes. The app is free with no in-app purchases, no subscriptions, and no ads. If a paid option ever lands, it would be a one-time purchase for the full game suite, paid theme packs, or a tip jar - never ads, coins, or aggressive subscriptions.

Will you add ads later?

No. That's a permanent constraint, not a launch promise.

Do I need an account?

No. GameDrawer is local-first by default. Sign in with Apple is optional and only enables CloudKit sync across your own devices.

What happens to my stats if I delete the app?

Local-only stats are erased with the app, the same as any other iOS app. If you exported a JSON backup or used optional iCloud sync, you can restore on reinstall. There is no server we operate that would still have your data.

What devices does GameDrawer run on?

iPhone running iOS 17 or later. iPad layout will follow.

How do I get help?

Email support. We read everything. If you're reporting a bug, mention your iOS version and what you were doing when it happened.
