Release Log · v1.0

Updates

A running log of what shipped, when, and why. Updated alongside each App Store release.

  • v1.4 · June 2026 Two word games join the drawer: Five Letter and Word Grid.

    Five Letter. Guess a five-letter word in six tries. Each guess colors every tile - green for the right letter in the right spot, yellow for a letter that belongs elsewhere - and the keyboard tracks what you've ruled out. A shared Daily word plus Unlimited for as many rounds as you like. Guesses are checked against a 15,000-word list; answers come from a pool of 1,000 common words. Resume support, stats, and Video Mode.

    Word Grid. Trace a path through adjacent letters on a 4×4 board to build words - longer words score more. Swipe across tiles to spell. Two modes: Timed against the clock or Relaxed with no pressure. Found words stay listed as you play, on a side rail on wider screens. Every board is generated to guarantee a fair set of findable words. Resume support, stats, and Video Mode.

    Home. Five Letter and Word Grid are now playable tiles on the home screen, and the Video Mode control moved onto the home screen with an inline enable switch and placement picker.

  • v1.3 · May 2026 FreeCell, Solitaire, new home screen, and in-progress save state.

    FreeCell. 8-column tableau, 4 free cells, 4 foundations. Tap or drag to move cards; multi-card sequences are capped by the standard supermove rule. Enter any deal number from 1 to 32,000 or pick Easy, Medium, Hard, or Expert at random. Undo, restart-this-deal, loss detection, win cascade animation, Video Mode across all six PiP zones, and stats.

    Klondike Solitaire. 7-column tableau, draw 1, 2, or 3 cards from the stock. Drag cards and sequences, double-tap to auto-send to foundation, auto-complete sweeps the remaining cards when the position is clear. Undo, restart, Video Mode, and stats.

    New home screen. A 3-column grid of game icons replaces the accordion drawer. Tap a tile to expand it: it scales up while the others compress into a horizontal strip. A detail panel below shows mode chips and a link to that game's stats. Tap anywhere else to collapse.

    Save state. All six games now save mid-session automatically. Close the app, come back, and a resume prompt appears. Decline and you start fresh - your save is cleared, nothing is lost permanently.

  • v1.2 · May 2026 Video Mode, Sudoku, and in-progress save state.

    Video Mode — all four games. Keep a video running in Picture-in-Picture while you play. Pick from six positions: large top, large bottom, or any of the four corners. Every game layout reflows around your video — controls stay visible, nothing gets buried. Switch it off and the layout returns to exactly what it was. Minesweeper, Merge, Nonogram, and Sudoku all support it.

    Sudoku. 9×9 classic logic. 6,000 curated puzzles across Easy, Medium, Hard, and Extreme. Pencil notes, single-step undo, and two modes: Free (wrong placements show in red, you keep going) or Lives (three mistakes ends the game). Complete a row, column, or 3×3 box and those cells briefly glow. Place the last of a number and the pad button bounces and strikes through — no more counting to see if there are any left.

    Save state. In-progress Sudoku puzzles save automatically. Come back after closing the app and pick up exactly where you left off. Completed puzzles are tracked across sessions so you won't see the same board twice.

    Polish. Lives counters now use hearts consistently across every game. Timer and lives chips lift clear of the board in large Video Mode zones. Compact control bars are consistently sized across all games.

  • v1.1 · May 11, 2026 Nonogram joins the cabinet. New drawer-style home screen. Per-game color palettes.

    Nonogram. A new picross-style logic puzzle. Fill the squares that match the row and column hints. Four sizes from 5×5 up to 20×20. Marks let you note squares you know are empty so you don't lose track. Each puzzle you solve gets saved as a thumbnail in a "Solved" gallery linked from the stats card, with the best clear time for each one.

    The Drawer. The home screen is now a vertical stack of drawers - one per game - in their own brand color. Tap a drawer and the face flips down to reveal mode chips: Easy / Medium / Hard for Minesweeper, 2048 / Infinite for Merge, the four sizes for Nonogram. Tap a chip and you drop straight into that mode. Tap anywhere outside the open drawer to close it.

    Theme-aware palettes. Each preset now picks one of two catalogue palettes based on its mood. Forest, Navy, Maroon, and the other sophisticated presets use a muted set (sage, dust navy, sienna, bronze). Dracula, Voltage, Bubblegum, and the other playful presets use a saturated set (vivid blue, spring green, hot red, sun amber). Each game pins to a stable slot so the cabinet reads as distinct blocks under any theme.

    Polish. Cleaner Merge mode labels in the toolbar ("2048 · Classic" and "Infinite · Endless" replace the wordier originals). Nonogram swipe detection got a deadzone restored so a glancing finger no longer marks adjacent rows. Nonogram board now has a sharp dark border with breathing room around the hint numbers, and the layout stays the same physical size from 5×5 to 20×20.

  • v1.0.1 · May 8, 2026 Polish pass: Minesweeper end-game choreography, Merge auto-spawn, per-app Classic theme.

    Minesweeper end-of-game animations. Lose, and the remaining mines now reveal as a wave radiating outward from the cell you tapped. Wrong flags pop afterward, then the end card slides in. Win, and confetti fires for a beat before the end card lands. A new "Animations" toggle lives in Settings under FEEL (the section that used to be called HAPTICS, since it now covers both subtle-feedback knobs). Default on. The system Reduce Motion setting still wins.

    Merge starts populated. Opening Merge used to drop you onto an empty grid with a hidden "swipe to start" trick. The board now spawns its initial two tiles immediately, so the first thing you see is something to play with.

    Classic preset is per-app now. Behind the scenes, the "Classic" theme slot used to be hardcoded as Chrome Diner across every Lauterstar app sharing DesignKit. Each app now registers its own Classic identity, so a fitness tracker is no longer wearing a games-app skin. GameDrawer keeps Chrome Diner. The five universal classics (Forest, Navy, Maroon, Walnut, Stone) are unchanged.

  • v1.0 · Coming soon GameDrawer opens. Minesweeper is the first cabinet.

    The opening volume - a clean drawer of classic logic games. v1.0 ships with Minesweeper alone, properly tuned, before adding more games as separate cabinets in the same drawer. Local-first by default, with optional Sign in with Apple iCloud sync layered on top.

    Minesweeper · Three difficulties (Easy 9×9 with 10 mines, Medium 16×16 with 40, Hard 16×30 with 99). First-tap safety guaranteed. Flood-fill auto-reveal for empty cells. Tap to reveal, long-press to flag. A clean win and loss state.

    Stats persistence · Best times per difficulty, total games played, win percentage, and a full record table. Stored locally via SwiftData so they survive restarts and OS updates.

    Themes · 34 presets across six DesignKit families (Classic, Sweet, Bright, Soft, Moody, Loud) plus a custom palette editor. The default identity is "Chrome Diner" - cream, teal, diner red, and brushed gunmetal.

    Sync & data · Optional iCloud sync via Sign in with Apple in the iCloud.com.lauterstar.gamekit private container. JSON stats export and import with a versioned schema. Sync is off by default.

    Haptics & sound · Polished feedback on flag, reveal, win, and loss. Fully toggleable in Settings.

    Privacy & performance · Apple privacy manifest declares no collected data and no tracking. Cold-start under one second. No popups. No required onboarding.
